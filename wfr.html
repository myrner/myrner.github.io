W.F.R. Wilderness First Responder

STEP ONE - SCENE SIZE-UP

  1. I'm #1! - Identify hazards
  2. What happened to you? - MOI
  3. None on me! - BSI
  4. Are there any more? - Number of patients
  5. How alive? - General impression of pateint condition

STEP TWO - INTRODUCTION AND CONSENT

Obstain consent. Make a human connection.

STEP THREE - LIFE THREATS

  • A. Airway
  • B. Breathing
  • C. Circulation
  • D. Decision on disabilty
  • E. Expose injuries

STEP FOUR - HEAD TO TOE, CSM

    Look, listen, feel, smell.
    Front to back.
    Maintain spinal stabilization if required.

STEP FIVE - VITALS

  • L. LOR (A+Ox4)VPU
  • H. HR Heart Rate
  • R. RR Respiratory Rate
  • S. SCTM Skin color, temp, moisture
  • B. BP Blood pressure
  • P. PERRL Pupils
  • T. Temperature

STEP SIX - HISTORY

  • S. Symptoms
  • A. Allergies
  • M. Medications
  • P. Pertinent history
  • L. Last in/out
  • E. Events

Focused Spinal Exam

  • A. Awake - A+Ox3 or 4
  • C. Clean - sober
  • D. Distractions - none
  • C. CSM - normal
  • S. Spine - free of pain or tenderness on palpation

S.O.A.P. NOTES

  • S. Summary/Subjective - What, who, where
  • O. Observations/Findings - Head to toe, vitals, History
  • A. Assessment - Problem list
  • P. Plan for treatment and evacuation
