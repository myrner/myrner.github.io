The Life and Legacy of Max Roach

  • 1924 - Born in Newland, North Carolina
  • 1934 - At the age of 10, plays drums in gospel bands
  • 1942 - Fresh out of high-school, fills in for Sonny Greer with the Duke Ellington Orchestra
  • 1943 - First professional recording supporting Coleman Hawkins
  • 1945 - Among the first drummers to play bebop style, Roach records with Charlie Parker
  • 1950-1953 - Studies classical percussion at the Manhattan School of Music (awarded an honorary doctorate in 1990)
  • 1952 - Records with Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, Bud Powell and Charles Mingus
  • 1954 - Records legendary album Clifford Brown and Max Roach
  • 1958 - Performs at Newport Jazz Festival with Dinah Washington
  • 1960 - Composes and records album We Insist to commemorate the hundredth anniversary of the Emancipation Proclamation
  • 1970s - Formed percusion orchestra M'Boom
  • 1980s - Performs solo concerts on drum set
  • 1990s - Continues to expand possibilities through musical theater and a series of free-improvisation duets with Cecil Taylor, Anthony Braxton, Archie Shepp and Abdullah Ibrahim
  • 2007 - Dies in Manhattan. He is survived by five children. In a funeral tribute, David Paterson, Lt. Gov of New York compared Max Roach to Harriet Tubman and Malcolm X
